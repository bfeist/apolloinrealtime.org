Apollo 13 in Real Time (2020)

In April of 2020, on the 50th anniversary, Apollo 13 in Real Time was launched for the general public.
This project took a small volunteer team over a year to create. It contains all of the mission's historical material, including 7200 hours of previously unreleased Mission Control audio.
All media is indexed by when it occurred during the mission. The result allows you "replay" the mission at the highest possible detail. No narrative. No exposition. Only raw source material retelling its own story.

The ill-fated Apollo 13 mission suffered an onboard explosion that crippled the spacecraft. Mission Control had to spring into action to rescue the crew against the odds. The historical recordings of these activities are made available here for the first time--in their entirety, providing new insight into the leadership and creativity of the men and women of Mission Control that earned them the Presidential Medal of Freedom.
